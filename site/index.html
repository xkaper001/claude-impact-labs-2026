The failure we fix

Two centers. One person.
Zero connection.

At the world's largest gathering, thousands go missing every day — mostly elderly pilgrims. The lost-and-found centers don't talk to each other.

Center A — “Found”

A confused elderly man arrives

No ID. Can't recall his name. A volunteer files a record at the Ramkund center. It stays right there.

Center B — “Searching”

His family is 3 km away

They describe him at a different center. They never see the record from Center A. The match exists — and stays invisible.

No cross-search. Hours pass. On a snan day, hours are dangerous.

The solution

One registry, searched everywhere.

A deterministic engine ranks the top matches across all centers. Claude handles language and vague descriptions. The volunteer always confirms.

Cross-center search

Every center reads and writes one shared registry. A found person anywhere becomes searchable everywhere — the core gap, closed.

Offline-first

Works when the network collapses near the ghats. PouchDB ⇄ CouchDB peer sync reconciles after.

Voice & multilingual

Mixed Hindi / Marathi / English in; family-facing phrasing out. The family just talks.

AI ranks, humans decide

Claude scores vague descriptions and explains each match — but it never confirms. A trained volunteer verifies before anyone is told. Privacy by design: no Aadhaar, no stored phone numbers.
